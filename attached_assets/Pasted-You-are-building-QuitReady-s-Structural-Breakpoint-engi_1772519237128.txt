0) NON-NEGOTIABLE PRINCIPLES (READ FIRST)

A) DEBT VS EXPENSES MUST BE IMPOSSIBLE TO CONFUSE

Users often pay expenses with a credit card. That does not make those expenses “debt.” They are still living/business expenses; the credit card minimum payment is the debt obligation. The UI must make this distinction unavoidable.

B) LIQUIDITY IS THE MAIN STORY — NOT TOTAL NET WORTH

The headline should be “Liquidity Line”: how long Tier 1 + Tier 2 capital covers the gap before the user enters Tier 3 (restricted) territory.
Tier 3 is emergency / last resort, never framed as “runway.”

C) DO NOT ENCOURAGE 401(k) OR RETIREMENT WITHDRAWALS

Retirement assets (401k/Traditional IRA) must be labeled “Restricted / Last Resort.”
No “great news you can use your 401k” vibe. The tone must be: “This is not liquid enough to leap without long-term damage.”

D) REPORT TONE = CO-PILOT / ADVISOR, CONVERSATIONAL, LAYMAN LANGUAGE

Every section explains what the numbers mean and why the section exists. Use warm, confident, practical language. No shaming, no hype, no recommendation like “do it / don’t do it.” It’s a mirror and a flashlight.

E) SCENARIO MATH MUST EXPAND

Report must show: revenue stress (-15%, -30%), ramp timing early/late (+/- 1/2/3 months), household shocks (partner job loss, new child), and cost levers.
