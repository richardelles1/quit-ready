2) REPLACE THE 3-PANEL FORM WITH A GUIDED QUESTION FLOW

Implement a step-by-step wizard with ONE primary question per screen (max 2 fields if needed).

Requirements:
	•	6–10 total steps
	•	Progress indicator (e.g., “Step 3 of 8”)
	•	Back button always works
	•	Each step has a short calm framing line (1 sentence)
	•	Avoid TurboTax form vibes (no huge stacks of inputs)

Suggested Step Structure (use this)

Step 1 — Foundation
Header: “Let’s start with your foundation.”
Q: “What does your life cost each month?” → monthly living expenses

Step 2 — Household Support
Header: “Now, your household baseline.”
Q: “Do you have partner income supporting the household?” toggle
If yes → partner monthly income input

Step 3 — Debt Load
Header: “Next: obligations that don’t disappear.”
Q: “What total debt balance continues if you leave?” → total debt balance
Follow-up on same step: “What are your monthly debt payments?” → monthly debt payments

Step 4 — Healthcare
Header: “Healthcare is usually the biggest delta.”
Q: “What happens to your coverage when you quit?”
Options:
	•	Covered by partner
	•	ACA marketplace
	•	COBRA
	•	Private plan
	•	No coverage (not recommended)
If ACA/COBRA/Private → ask monthly healthcare estimate
If partner → set estimate to 0 but show note “Partner coverage reduces exposure.”

Step 5 — Cash Cushion
Header: “Now: the capital you can actually reach.”
Q: “How much cash do you have?” → cash/HYSA

Step 6 — Investments
Header: “Let’s include what you could tap if needed.”
Q: “How much in taxable brokerage?” → brokerage
Optional “Advanced” toggle expands:
	•	Roth contributions
	•	Retirement accounts
	•	Home equity

Step 7 — Business Path
Header: “Now let’s model what life after quitting could look like.”
Q: “What best describes your work model?”
Options:
	•	Solo bootstrap
	•	Lean agency/service
	•	Product build
	•	Custom

Auto-populate baseline business cost.
If custom → input monthly business cost.

Step 8 — Revenue Expectation
Header: “Let’s pressure test your revenue expectations.”
Q: “What monthly revenue do you expect once stabilized?” → expected revenue

Step 9 — Ramp
Header: “Most transitions take longer than we want.”
Q: “How many months until revenue stabilizes?” → ramp months

Step 10 — Volatility
Header: “How swingy is this income likely to be?”
Q: “Expected volatility (%)” default 15, range 10–30

Then show CTA: “Run Stress Test”
